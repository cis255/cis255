About

A Glimpse into the History of TEDxSVSU

What is TED?

"TED is a nonprofit devoted to spreading ideas, usually in the form of short, powerful talks (18 minutes or less). TED began in 1984 as a conference where Technology, Entertainment and Design converged, and today covers almost all topics — from science to business to global issues — in more than 100 languages. Meanwhile, independently run TEDx events help share ideas in communities around the world." Source: www.ted.com. 

What is TEDx?

"TEDx was created in the spirit of TED's mission, "ideas worth spreading." It supports independent organizers who want to create a TED-like event in their own community." Source: www.ted.com.



What is TEDxSVSU?

TEDxSVSU is an independently organized TED-style event, a full day of local, national and international speakers, music and artistic presenters, and also sponsors and exhibitors who showcase novel displays and activities. The target audience is SVSU and the surrounding community, especially students, parents, families, staff, faculty, and administration.  Organizers and leaders of the event include SVSU students, faculty and staff. The project is supported by the SVSU Foundation Research Grant Program. 

When is it?

Come join the fun starting at 8 AM EST on Friday, September 30 at Saginaw Valley State University!

How can I attend?

Tickets are $25 and can be purchased over the phone or at the door. Call 989.964.4295 for more info!

Want to speak or volunteer?

Volunteers and speakers are unpaid, but they are guaranteed a free ticket, and a reserved seat in the audience when they are not volunteering. If you would like to speak or help the organizers prepare the event, email us at TEDxSVSU@gmail.com!

Exhibits

In addition to the event speakers, there will also be exhibits setup throughout. Exhibits are booth-style displays of novel local technology, entertainment and design implementations. Exhibitors can include sponsoring corporations, students or local technologists or artisans.  The purpose of exhibits is to give the audience a chance to see and experience things that they would not normally have exposure to either in daily life or even in college classes.
