What is TED?

"TED is a nonprofit devoted to spreading ideas, usually in the form of short, powerful talks (18 minutes or less). TED began in 1984 as a conference where Technology, Entertainment and Design converged, and today covers almost all topics — from science to business to global issues — in more than 100 languages." Source: www.ted.com.

What is TEDˣ?

TEDˣ is an offshoot of TED which supports the creation of TED-like events in an independent organizer's own community. TEDˣ was created in the spirit of TED's mission, "ideas worth spreading." You can read more here.

What is TEDˣSVSU?

TEDˣSVSU is a TEDˣ event hosted at SVSU: a full day of local, national, and international speakers, artistic presentations, and also display and activity showcasing.

Who can attend?

TEDˣSVSU is organized and led by SVSU students and faculty, but it is not exclusive to those affiliated with Saginaw Valley. Anyone is welcome to attend.

TED events contain two main attractions:

Speeches, 10-20 minute presentations given by volunteer speakers; and exhibits, booth-style displays of local-made technology, entertainment, and design implementations.

Dr. Clark's topic: From Concept to Reality: How a University is Created

Dr. Basil Clark is a Professor Emeritus of English at Saginaw Valley State University. He has his Ph.D. from Ohio State University and recently retired from SVSU after forty years of service. He taught a wide variety of English composition, language, and literature courses, with a particular interest in Old and Middle English literature. He is editor of the first volume of SVSU history, The Early and Formative Years, and contributing editor to the second, The Years of Growth and Maturity.



Judge Darnell Jackson's topic: The Value of Human Worth

Darnell Jackson is a Circuit Court Judge, informational speaker and nationwide presenter for the National Judicial Institute on Domestic Violence. He is the author of the acclaimed book The Steps of A Good Man, which provides practical and proven methods for dealing with the challenges and setbacks of life. His philosophy is, “I have an obligation to those who hear me speak to provide them with something of significance that they didn’t have before.”
